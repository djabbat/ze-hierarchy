I'm applying for a Flash Grant to build an open-source hardware experiment that tests whether the **physical age of manufacturing** affects collective behavior in identical random-walk robots.

## The Idea

120 identical bristlebots (vibration-driven robots), produced in two batches:
- **60 new** (made today) — bright LED, random walk
- **60 old** (made 30 days ago) — dim LED, follow the bright ones

The hypothesis: *new agents intervene (they have no world model), old agents monitor (they have built a model).* This creates spontaneous hierarchy: olds follow the news.

## Why It Matters

1. **If true**: physical age (calendar time since manufacture) influences behavior without programming — a fundamentally new mechanism in swarm robotics.
2. **If false**: still an open-source 120-robot platform for $675 — cheapest multi-agent testbed in existence.
3. **Open hardware**: all schematics, code, and data will be CC-BY.
